===Contemporary press clippings:===
A well-known Port Adelaide unionist, Mr. Leonard Carson, died at his residence, Dundas street, Yatala, on Thursday. Deceased. who was highly popular with all who knew him, was a member of both, the Port Adelaide branch of the Waterside Workers' Federation and the local branch of the Drivers' Union, besides which he took an active interest in lodge work. In his unions, particularly the drivers', he always took a prominent part, and was one whose views were always listened to. He was a past president of the Drivers' Union, and was for a long time an executive officer. "He was one of the finest trade unionists I have met," was the tribute paid on Friday by the local secretary of the Drivers Union (Mr . J. E. Stephens). The deceased was a past-president of the Port Adelaide branch of the A.N.A., and was also connected with the Alberton branch of the Loyal Orange Lodge. The funeral will take place this afternoon. 
Daily Herald September 4 1920
